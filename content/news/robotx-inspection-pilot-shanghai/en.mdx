RobotX has launched a multi-robot inspection pilot on an active manufacturing
floor in Shanghai. The deployment runs across two shifts per day and covers
routine patrol routes, anomaly triage, and operator handoff back into the
plant's existing operations workflow.

## Scope

The pilot pairs a quadruped patrol unit with fixed sensor stations at high-risk
equipment lines. Together they capture thermal, vibration, and visual data on
each pass, flag deviations against a baseline, and push exceptions into the
plant's shift dashboard so operators can act without leaving their existing
tools.

## Why it matters

Inspection pilots usually stall at the integration layer — the robot works in
isolation, but never quite lands inside the customer's operations loop. This
deployment was designed around the handoff from day one: the robot's output is
a structured exception, not a video feed, and the operator stays in charge of
the decision.

## What we are watching

Three metrics define success for the pilot: coverage consistency across shifts,
false-positive rate on anomaly flags, and time-to-action once an exception lands
on the shift dashboard. Early results will inform the cadence of the next two
inspection sites going live later this year.
