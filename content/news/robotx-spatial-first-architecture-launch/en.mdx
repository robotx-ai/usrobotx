RobotX today introduced its Spatial-First Architecture, a foundation layer for
field robotics that treats three-dimensional perception as the primary interface
between a robot and its environment. The architecture brings together real-time
3D SLAM, multimodal fusion, and a vendor-agnostic operating layer designed to
run consistently across quadruped, wheeled, and humanoid platforms.

## Why spatial-first

Most deployed robots today are built around a video-first pipeline. That
approach breaks quickly in environments with weak networks, poor lighting, or
cluttered geometry — the kinds of conditions industrial customers actually
operate in. Spatial-First inverts the default: perception, planning, and control
all reason directly over 3D structure, with camera semantics layered on top
rather than underneath.

## What ships first

The initial release covers three surfaces:

- **RobotX OS** — a thin, hardware-independent operating layer that normalizes
  sensor streams, motion primitives, and safety envelopes across supported
  platforms.
- **Spatial perception stack** — 3D LiDAR SLAM with camera-semantic fusion and
  IMU-aided localization, tuned for weak-network and edge-only deployments.
- **Skill packs** — modular vertical behaviors — patrol, cleaning, logistics,
  and inspection — that ship pre-integrated and improve from fleet data.

## Availability

The first Spatial-First deployments roll out with existing RobotX customers
across inspection and logistics this quarter. A broader developer preview is
planned for later in the year.
